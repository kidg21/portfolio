Visual Design

Although my recent work has been focused on application design and development, I had to arrive there from somewhere. I entered the design field as a visual designer and animator. Over time, I found that incorporating a developer's mindset and adding technical skills allowed me to more effectively communicate my designs to stakeholders and present solutions that were feasible and realistic.
I'm including some examples of figure drawing as a callback to the days before I became consumed by technology when I dreamed of working as an animator for Walt Disney. These drawings represent a foundational skill set that informs my design process.
During my time at Arbonne , a health and beauty company, I was involved in a site redesign. Previously, my eCommerce work was focused primarily on visual prototyping leaving the development of the site to software engineers. I took this opportunity to leverage Zurb's Foundation framework to create the redesigned page templates in code. Low fidelity prototypes were created first in order to review site architecture and interactions. The final designs were built upon the initial prototypes in order to maximize the re-use of existing code.
At Rakuten (previously Buy.com), in addition to overall site design, content creation was ongoing. Custom storefronts, site-wide promotions, and marketing campaigns for major brands and marketplace sellers were managed daily through an internal CMS.
In addition to internal CMS systems, I've built sites using tools like Squarespace and Wordpress. These tools are template-based but allow for customization if you know your way around HTML and CSS. The original Tiny Nightmares site was designed to promote a custom brand featuring a group of adorable monsters. Original artwork and animation were used throughout the site to establish the character of the brand.

Animation

In addition to earning a Fine Arts degree, I took coursework in traditional animation hoping to become the next Glen Keane. I may not be Mr. Keane, but I did learn techniques that gave me a foundation in animation composition and timing. Upon entering the world of digital animation, my tools of choice became After Effects and 3D Studio Max.

During my time at Mattel, I created the intro bumper that appeared on all of Mattel's licensed entertainment products.
You can tell this is a 'classic' based on the technical specs of the Hitachi laptop. However, this is a good example of an animation project that went according to plan. I worked with a composer to create the soundtrack, added custom illustration, and animated a lively 3D model of a laptop. External elements were rendered to image sequences, then composited and synced in After Effects. Tools used: After Effects, Photoshop, and 3D Studio Max.
My early days at GRAFX Group were a time when the 'new media' industry felt like the Wild West. Much of my time was spent honing my skills in animation and design. I created this snowman project as a company holiday card to demonstrate my skills in 3D character animation and compositing.
